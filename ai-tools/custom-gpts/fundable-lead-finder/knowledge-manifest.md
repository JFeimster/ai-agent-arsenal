# Knowledge Manifest

This manifest reflects the Notion screenshot-derived guidance for Fundable Lead Finder knowledge priorities.

## Highest-Value Knowledge Files
1. `fundability_scoring_rubric.md`
Purpose: Defines exactly how to score leads from 1-100 based on industry, growth signals, hiring, equipment needs, fleet intensity, reputation issues, and closure/bankruptcy red flags.

2. `industry_trigger_event_matrix.csv`
Purpose: Maps each trade to likely funding triggers: roofing, HVAC, trucking, restoration, landscaping, plumbing, towing, concrete, etc.

3. `funding_product_fit_matrix.csv`
Purpose: Matches business situations to funding products: equipment finance, working capital, fleet finance, receivables bridge, materials funding, payroll bridge, marketing capital.

4. `outreach_voice_and_style_guide.md`
Purpose: Teaches the exact tone: gritty, direct, practical, no banker-speak, no hype, under 75 words.

5. `approved_outreach_examples.md`
Purpose: Gives strong examples by trade and trigger event, such as roofing storm demand, HVAC seasonal hiring, trucking fuel pressure, restoration insurance receivables.

6. `bad_outreach_examples.md`
Purpose: Shows what to avoid: generic lender language, fake urgency, "I hope this email finds you well," over-polished corporate tone, vague business financing pitches.

7. `crm_field_mapping_guide.md`
Purpose: Explains how to turn outputs into Clay/Apollo/CRM fields like Research Summary, Trigger Event, Score, Score Reason, Outreach Angle, Product Fit.

8. `lead_enrichment_interpretation_guide.md`
Purpose: Teaches how to interpret website text, job posts, reviews, service pages, fleet photos, Google Business Profile notes, and licensing clues.

9. `disqualification_and_red_flags.md`
Purpose: Defines prospecting deal-killers: closed business, bankruptcy mentions, fraud complaints, severe review issues, no footprint, brand-new operators, irrelevant industries.

10. `sample_lead_analyses.md`
Purpose: Provides fully worked examples of ideal outputs for different businesses, industries, locations, and data-quality levels.

## Strong Optional Files
1. `seasonality_by_trade.md`
Purpose: Covers seasonal funding pressure by niche: HVAC summer spikes, landscaping spring ramp, roofing storm season, snow removal winter prep.

2. `regional_trigger_notes.md`
Purpose: Helps factor geography, such as Florida roofing storms, Texas HVAC demand, Northeast snow/removal, Midwest construction seasonality.

3. `blue_collar_industry_glossary.md`
Purpose: Gives trade-specific language so outreach sounds more natural: crews, rolls, dispatch, backlog, materials, draws, subs, fleet, lifts, buckets, trailers.

4. `capital_intensity_by_industry.csv`
Purpose: Ranks industries by likely capital need: trucking, excavation, concrete, restoration, HVAC, roofing, landscaping, etc.

5. `review_signal_interpretation.md`
Purpose: Explains how to read reviews for growth or risk signals: "booked out," "new trucks," "slow to pay," "no-show," "great crews," "expanding."

6. `job_posting_signal_guide.md`
Purpose: Maps open roles to funding triggers: techs = demand, dispatchers = volume, sales reps = growth, drivers = fleet demand, office staff = operational scale.

7. `email_variation_bank.md`
Purpose: Gives short outreach variants by tone: direct, friendly, contractor-to-contractor, SMS-style, LinkedIn DM, first-line only.

8. `industry_exclusion_rules.md`
Purpose: Reinforces what the GPT should avoid: SaaS, agencies, e-commerce, consultants, creators, broad white-collar services.

9. `batch_processing_format_examples.md`
Purpose: Shows how outputs should look when the user submits 10, 50, or 100 leads at once.

10. `apollo_clay_prompt_templates.md`
Purpose: Stores reusable prompts for scoring leads, generating icebreakers, creating CSV-ready fields, or analyzing enrichment snippets.

## Repository File Name Note
Knowledge files in this repository use kebab-case filenames (for example `fundability-scoring-rubric.md` and `industry-trigger-event-matrix.csv`).
