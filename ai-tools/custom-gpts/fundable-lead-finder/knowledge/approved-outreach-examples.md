# Approved Outreach Examples

**File:** `approved_outreach_examples.md`
**Owner GPT:** Fundable Lead Finder
**Purpose:** A reference bank of strong, on-voice outreach messages organized by trade and trigger event. Use these as patterns to adapt — not to copy verbatim. Every example is under 75 words, opens on the operator's world, names one specific product, and ends with a low-friction CTA.

> **How to use:** Match the lead's trade + dominant trigger to the closest example, then rewrite with the real business name and any known specifics. Keep the structure; swap the details.

---

## Roofing

**Trigger: Storm/hail demand surge**
> Storm season's clearly keeping you busy. The catch is usually materials and crew payroll going out the door before the insurance checks land. We do receivables bridge funding built for that exact gap — keeps jobs moving while you wait on claims. Want me to send a quick example?

**Trigger: Hiring crews / scaling**
> Saw you're adding crews heading into peak season. Smart — but that's usually when payroll outruns cash flow on the bigger jobs. We've got working capital that covers the gap while the season pays you back. Open to seeing numbers?

---

## HVAC

**Trigger: Seasonal demand + inventory stocking**
> Summer's about to hit and most shops are scrambling to stock units and keep installers busy. Tying up cash in inventory is brutal right before peak. We do inventory and working capital lines built for HVAC seasonality. Want the 60-second version?

**Trigger: Adding service vans**
> Looks like you're growing the install side. Adding vans out of pocket can choke cash right when you need it for payroll. Equipment financing lets you put the trucks to work now and keep your cash free. Worth a quick look?

---

## Trucking & Logistics

**Trigger: Fuel float + slow broker pay**
> If you're like most carriers right now, fuel's eating margin and brokers are paying on their own sweet time. Freight factoring gets you paid on loads the day you run them instead of waiting weeks. Want me to show how it'd cash-flow for your lanes?

**Trigger: Truck/trailer purchase**
> Adding trucks for a new contract? Paying cash for iron is the fastest way to get tight before the freight even starts paying. We do fleet financing so you put trucks on the road now and pay as they earn. Open to it?
