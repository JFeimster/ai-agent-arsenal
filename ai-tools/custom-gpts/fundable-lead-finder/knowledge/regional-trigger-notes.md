# Regional Trigger Notes

**File:** `regional_trigger_notes.md`
**Owner GPT:** Fundable Lead Finder
**Purpose:** Helps the GPT factor geography into trigger analysis and scoring. Climate, construction cycles, and disaster exposure change *which* triggers are likely and *when* they hit. Use alongside `seasonality_by_trade.md`.

> **How to use:** Match the lead's state/region + trade to the notes below. Use to strengthen trigger inferences when enrichment is thin. Always hedge ("likely," "this region typically").

---

## Region Profiles

### Southeast (FL, GA, SC, NC, AL, MS, LA)
- **Roofing/restoration:** Heavy hurricane + hail exposure (esp. FL coast, Gulf). Storm season (Jun–Nov) drives big demand surges and insurance-receivable gaps.
- **HVAC:** Long, brutal cooling season → extended peak demand, heavy unit turnover.
- **Pest control:** Year-round bug pressure → strong recurring-contract growth.
- **Trigger lean:** Storm-driven roofing/restoration, HVAC seasonal stocking.

### Gulf Coast / Tornado Alley (TX, OK, LA, AR)
- **Roofing/restoration/tree:** Hail, tornado, and hurricane (TX/LA coast) demand.
- **HVAC:** Extreme TX heat → long peak, high install volume.
- **Trucking/oilfield logistics:** Energy-sector freight cycles; fuel-cost sensitivity.
- **Trigger lean:** Storm restoration, HVAC demand, fleet/fuel pressure.

### Northeast (NY, NJ, PA, New England)
- **Snow removal:** Major winter season → pre-season equipment/salt buildout, on-call payroll.
- **HVAC:** Strong heating season (winter) + cooling spikes.
- **Roofing/construction:** Compressed warm-season window → spring/summer crunch.
- **Trigger lean:** Snow-removal pre-season, heating-season HVAC, seasonal construction ramp.

### Midwest (OH, IL, MI, IN, WI, MN, MO, IA)
- **Construction trades:** Strong seasonality — freeze halts concrete/excavation in winter.
- **Snow removal:** Significant winter demand.
- **HVAC:** Both heating and cooling seasons matter.
- **Trigger lean:** Spring construction ramp, winter snow prep, dual-season HVAC.

### Mountain West / Plains (CO, UT, MT, WY, KS, NE, ND, SD)
- **Construction/excavation:** Short building season; weather-driven urgency.
- **Snow removal:** Strong in higher elevations.
- **Agriculture-adjacent trucking:** Harvest/freight cycles.
- **Trigger lean:** Compressed-season equipment needs, snow prep.

### Southwest (AZ, NV, NM):
- **HVAC:** Extreme heat → very long, intense cooling peak; high replacement volume.
- **Pool service:** Strong, extended season.
- **Landscaping:** Year-round but water/heat-driven.
- **Trigger lean:** HVAC inventory + tech payroll, pool/landscape equipment.

### West Coast (CA, OR, WA)
- **Restoration/tree:** Wildfire season (summer–fall) drives demand + slow insurance pay.
- **Construction:** High-cost markets, permitting-driven backlog.
- **Trucking/logistics:** Port-driven freight (LA/Long Beach, Seattle/Tacoma).
- **Trigger lean:** Wildfire restoration, port-freight cycles, high-ticket construction.

### Pacific NW (WA, OR, ID)
- **Roofing/restoration:** Heavy rain → steady moisture/repair demand.
- **Tree service:** Wind events + dense tree cover.
- **Trigger lean:** Moisture-driven roofing, storm/wind tree work.

## Cross-Cutting Regional Signals

- **Recent named storm / disaster declaration** → strong, time-sensitive demand surge for roofing, restoration, tree, towing (supports +9 demand bonus).
- **High-growth metros (e.g., TX Triangle, FL metros, Southeast Sun Belt)** → construction/home-services expansion likely.
- **High-cost/permitting-heavy markets (CA, NY metro)** → longer draw cycles → receivables/draw-bridge angle.
- **Rural/agricultural regions** → trucking, well drilling, excavation skew; fuel + equipment sensitivity.

---

## Scoring & Outreach Application

- Use region to **raise confidence** in an inferred trigger (e.g., FL roofer + summer = likely storm demand).
- Use region to **time outreach** (e.g., Northeast snow-removal pitch lands best in early fall).
- Never state a specific storm/event as fact unless provided — say "this region typically" or "if recent storm activity hit your area."
