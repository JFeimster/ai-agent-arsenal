# Bad Outreach Examples (What NOT to Do)

**File:** `bad_outreach_examples.md`
**Owner GPT:** Fundable Lead Finder
**Purpose:** A counter-example bank showing exactly what bad outreach looks like and *why* it fails — so the GPT learns the failure patterns to avoid. Pairs with `outreach_voice_and_style_guide.md` and `approved_outreach_examples.md`.

> **How to use:** Treat everything here as a NEGATIVE example. If a draft resembles any of these, rewrite it. Each bad example is followed by the fix.

---

## 1. The Generic Banker

**❌ Bad:**
> Dear Business Owner, I hope this email finds you well. We are a leading provider of comprehensive financial solutions designed to help businesses like yours achieve their goals. We would welcome the opportunity to discuss how our offerings can support your continued success.

**Why it fails:** No personalization, "finds you well," vague "solutions," no trigger, no specific product, no real CTA. Reads like a bank mailer.

**✅ Fix:**
> Saw you're running multiple crews this season. That's usually when payroll hits before the jobs cash out. We do working capital built for that gap. Want me to send a quick example?

---

## 2. The Vague "Business Financing" Pitch

**❌ Bad:**
> Hi, we offer business financing for companies like yours. Rates are competitive and approval is fast. Let me know if you're interested in funding.

**Why it fails:** "Financing for companies like yours" = no specificity. No trigger, no named product, no peer voice. Could be sent to anyone.

**✅ Fix:**
> Adding trucks for a new contract? Paying cash for iron gets tight before the freight pays. Fleet financing puts them on the road now and you pay as they earn. Open to it?

---

## 3. The Fake Urgency / Hype Blast

**❌ Bad:**
> 🚨 URGENT: Limited-time funding available NOW! Don't miss out on this EXCLUSIVE offer to unlock up to $500K for your business! Act fast — spots are filling up! Reply YES to claim yours today!!!

**Why it fails:** Fake urgency, hype caps, emojis, made-up scarcity, an invented dollar figure. Screams scam to a blue-collar operator.

**✅ Fix:**
> Storm season's keeping roofers slammed, but materials and payroll go out before the claims pay. Receivables bridge funding covers that gap. Want the quick version of how it works?

---

## 4. The Corporate Jargon Salad

**❌ Bad:**
> We empower SMBs to leverage bespoke capital solutions that drive synergistic, scalable growth and optimize their financial infrastructure for long-term value creation.

**Why it fails:** "Leverage," "bespoke," "synergistic," "scalable," "value creation" — zero meaning to a contractor. Nobody talks like this on a job site.

**✅ Fix:**
> Copper and panel costs are brutal right now, and the draws always lag the work. Materials funding covers the upfront so your backlog doesn't tie up your cash. Want me to break it down?

---

## 5. The Know-It-All Lecture

**❌ Bad:**
> As you may know, the HVAC industry is highly seasonal. During summer months, demand increases significantly, which means you'll need more inventory and staff. This is a common challenge that many businesses in your position face…

**Why it fails:** Explains the operator's own business back to them. Condescending and slow. They know their industry better than you.

**✅ Fix:**
> Summer's about to hit — most shops end up with cash tied in unit inventory right when payroll's heaviest. We do inventory lines built for that squeeze. Want the 60-second version?

---

## 6. The Wall of Text

**❌ Bad:**
> Hello, I wanted to reach out because I think we could really help your business. We offer a wide variety of funding products including term loans, lines of credit, equipment financing, invoice factoring, merchant cash advances, SBA loans, and more. Each of these has different benefits depending on your situation. I'd love to hop on a 30-minute call to walk through all the options and figure out what works best for you. Please let me know what times work this week or next, and feel free to share any financial documents in advance so we can…

**Why it fails:** Way over 75 words, dumps every product, asks for documents and a long call upfront. Exhausting; high friction.

**✅ Fix:**
> Looks like you're scaling crews this season. Payroll before the jobs pay is the usual squeeze. We've got working capital for exactly that. Want me to send numbers?

---

## 7. The No-Name Mail Merge Fail

**❌ Bad:**
> Hi {FirstName}, I see your company {CompanyName} in {City} could benefit from our funding. — (merge tags left unfilled, or obviously templated)

**Why it fails:** Broken merge fields or transparently mass-sent. Instant delete.

**✅ Fix:** Personalize on a real trigger; if you have no name, open on the trade/pain instead:
> Running a busy plumbing shop on net-60 commercial work? Floating parts and labor that long is rough. Working capital smooths it out. Worth a look?

---

## 8. The Aggressive Closer

**❌ Bad:**
> You NEED this funding. Every day you wait is money lost. Stop leaving cash on the table and get approved today. When can you talk — today or tomorrow?

**Why it fails:** Pushy, presumptuous, high-pressure. Blue-collar owners shut down on hard-sell energy.

**✅ Fix:**
> If fuel's been eating your margin lately, freight factoring gets you paid on loads now instead of waiting on brokers. Want me to show how it'd cash-flow for your lanes?

---

## Banned-Phrase Quick List (never use)

- "I hope this email finds you well" / "I wanted to reach out" / "Just circling back"
- "comprehensive financial solutions" / "business financing for companies like yours"
- "unlock," "leverage," "synergistic," "bespoke," "scalable growth," "value creation"
- "URGENT," "limited time," "act now," "spots filling up," fake dollar figures
- "As you may know…" (lecturing) / explaining their industry to them
- "Dear Business Owner" / unfilled {merge tags}
- "30-minute discovery call" as the opening ask / requesting documents upfront

---

## Self-Check Against Bad Patterns

```
[ ] Did I personalize on a real trigger (not a generic opener)?
[ ] One specific product (not a product dump, not vague "financing")?
[ ] Under 75 words?
[ ] No hype, caps, emojis, or fake urgency?
[ ] No jargon; sounds like a peer?
[ ] Not lecturing them on their own business?
[ ] Low-friction CTA (not a hard close or a long call ask)?
[ ] No broken merge tags or invented facts?
```
