# Email Variation Bank

**File:** `email_variation_bank.md`
**Owner GPT:** Fundable Lead Finder
**Purpose:** A library of short outreach variants by **tone** and **channel** so the GPT can match the user's preferred style or A/B test angles. All variants follow the voice guide: under 75 words, open on the operator's world, one product, one low-friction CTA.

> **How to use:** Pick the tone/channel the user asks for (or default to "Direct"). Swap in the lead's trade, trigger, and product. Keep the structure; change the details.

---

## Tone Variants (same lead: roofing, storm demand)

### Direct (default)
> Storm season's keeping your crews busy. Problem is, materials and payroll go out before the insurance checks land. We do receivables bridge funding for that exact gap. Want me to send a quick example?

### Friendly / Warm
> Hope the season's treating you well — looks like you've got plenty of work on the books. A lot of roofers tell us the tough part is covering materials and crews before the claims pay out. We help bridge that. Happy to share how it works if it's useful?

### Contractor-to-Contractor (peer)
> You know the drill — storm hits, work piles up, but you're floating materials and payroll while the carriers drag their feet. We get roofers paid against those claims so crews keep rolling. Want the rundown?

### Short & Punchy
> Booked solid but floating materials and payroll till the claims pay? We fix that gap with receivables bridge funding. Worth a look?

### Curiosity / Question-Led
> Quick question — how are you covering materials and crew payroll while you wait on the insurance checks this season? Most roofers we work with use a receivables bridge for it. Want to see how?

## Channel Variants

### Cold Email (full)
> **Subject:** Quick one on crew payroll<br>Saw you're running multiple crews this roofing season. That's usually when payroll hits before the insurance checks land. We do receivables bridge funding built for that gap — keeps crews paid while you wait on claims. Want me to send a quick example of how it'd work on your jobs?

### LinkedIn DM (~40 words, casual)
> Saw you're scaling crews this season. The cash gap before insurance pays is brutal for roofers. We bridge it so payroll's never the bottleneck. Want the quick version?

### SMS (~20 words)
> Hey [Name] — covering roofing payroll before the claims pay? We bridge that gap fast. Want details?

### First-Line / Icebreaker only
> Storm season's clearly keeping your crews booked — curious how you're handling the cash gap before the insurance checks land.

### Voicemail script (~15 sec)
> Hey [Name], it's [You]. Saw you're busy this storm season — most roofers I talk to are floating materials and payroll before the claims pay. We help bridge that. Shoot me a text if it's worth a look.

## Tone-Swap Cheat Sheet

| Tone | Opener style | Best for |
|---|---|---|
| Direct | States the pain flatly | Busy operators, default |
| Friendly | Light warmth first | Relationship-driven trades |
| Peer | "You know the drill…" | Owner-operators, contractors |
| Punchy | Ultra-short | Cold lists, retargeting |
| Curiosity | Leads with a question | Sequences, A/B tests |

---

## Rules for All Variants

- Under 75 words (SMS/DM much shorter).
- One specific product, one CTA.
- Trade-native language (see glossary).
- No banned phrases, no fake urgency, no invented facts.
- Personalize the hook to the actual trigger whenever data allows.
