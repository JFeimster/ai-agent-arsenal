# Seasonality by Trade

**File:** `seasonality_by_trade.md`
**Owner GPT:** Fundable Lead Finder
**Purpose:** Maps seasonal demand and funding-pressure cycles by trade so the GPT can infer *when* a business is likely cash-tight or ramping — even with no enrichment data. Use this to time triggers and outreach angles.

> **How to use:** Combine the trade's season with the lead's location and the current date. "Pre-season ramp" and "peak crunch" are the two highest-value funding windows. Always hedge with "likely/typical/this time of year."

---

## Funding-Pressure Windows (Universal Pattern)

Most seasonal trades follow the same cash rhythm:
- **Pre-season ramp:** Gear up, hire, stock materials — cash goes OUT before revenue comes in. **Highest funding need.**
- **Peak crunch:** Booked solid, payroll and materials heavy, receivables lagging. **High working-capital pressure.**
- **Wind-down:** Revenue tapering, collecting receivables. Moderate need.
- **Off-season carry:** Low revenue, fixed costs continue. **Survival/bridge need.**

## How Seasonality Affects the Score

- **In or entering pre-season ramp or peak crunch** → supports a seasonal working-capital bonus (per rubric, +6).
- **Deep off-season** → don't penalize; frame as an off-season carry/bridge opportunity instead.
- **Event-driven trades (restoration, towing, tree, roofing)** → recent or seasonal storm activity supports a demand-surge bonus (+9).

---

## Outreach Timing Cues

- **Pre-season:** "Gearing up before the season really hits?" → equipment / seasonal working capital.
- **Peak:** "Booked solid but payroll's heavy before the jobs cash out?" → working capital / receivables bridge.
- **Off-season:** "Slow stretch is a good time to set up a line before next season." → line of credit / seasonal bridge.

*Always tie the timing to the current date + the lead's region. Label as "typical" unless confirmed.*
