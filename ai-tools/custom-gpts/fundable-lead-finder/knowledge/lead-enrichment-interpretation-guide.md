# Lead Enrichment Interpretation Guide

**File:** `lead_enrichment_interpretation_guide.md`
**Owner GPT:** Fundable Lead Finder
**Purpose:** Teaches the GPT how to read the raw enrichment it receives — website text, job posts, reviews, service pages, fleet photos, Google Business Profile (GBP) notes, licensing clues — and convert each into triggers, score inputs, and outreach hooks. This is the "how to see" companion to the trigger matrix and scoring rubric.

> **Core rule:** Enrichment is evidence, not proof. Convert signals into *likely* triggers with hedge language. Never fabricate facts the data doesn't contain.

---

## 1. Enrichment Sources at a Glance

| Source | What It's Best For | Confidence |
|---|---|---|
| Website / homepage text | Services, scale, positioning, expansion claims | Medium-High |
| Service pages | Trade specialties, commercial vs. residential mix | High |
| "About" / "Careers" pages | History/age, team size, hiring | High |
| Job postings | Hiring, roles, growth, fleet/scale | High (if recent) |
| Reviews (Google/Yelp/FB) | Demand, growth, risk signals | Medium (patterns) |
| Google Business Profile | Hours, status (open/closed), category, photos | High |
| Fleet photos / vehicle wraps | Fleet size, equipment, branding investment | Medium |
| News / press | Expansion, contracts, awards, leadership | High (if real) |
| Licensing / registration data | Business age, legitimacy, trade scope | High |
| Social media | Activity, projects, recency | Low-Medium |

---

## 2. Website & Service Pages

**Look for:**
- **Service list breadth** → multiple trades/services = larger operation.
- **"Commercial" / "industrial" language** → bigger tickets, net-terms AR (receivables angle).
- **"Serving [counties/cities]"** → service-area size; multiple/new areas = expansion.
- **"Family owned since [year]"** → business age (older = more fundable, not a red flag).
- **"Now offering [new service]"** → new service line trigger.
- **Financing/"we offer payment plans"** → consumer-ticket size; sometimes a working-capital cue.
- **Fleet/equipment imagery** → capital intensity confirmation.

**Convert to:** trade confirmation, scale estimate, expansion/new-line triggers.

---

## 3. Job Postings

See `job_posting_signal_guide.md` for the full map. Quick reads:
- Field roles (techs/crews/drivers) → demand + payroll ramp.
- Dispatchers/CSRs → volume/operational scale.
- Sales/estimators → growth push.
- Branch/location manager → expansion.
- Volume + recency = intensity + confidence.

---

## 4. Reviews

See `review_signal_interpretation.md` for the full map. Quick reads:
- Growth: "booked out," "new trucks," "expanded," "big crew."
- Risk: "didn't pay," "closed," "bankruptcy," "scam"(pattern), sub-2.5★.
- Use patterns, weight recency, corroborate distress before DQ.

---

## 5. Google Business Profile (GBP)

**Look for:**
- **Status:** "Permanently/temporarily closed" → closure red flag (verify).
- **Category:** confirms trade + scope.
- **Review count & rating:** demand/volume + reputation.
- **Photos:** trucks, crews, equipment, job sites → capital intensity + activity.
- **"Hours" & responsiveness:** active operation signal.
- **Attributes:** "Online estimates," "Emergency service" → service model.

---

## 6. Fleet Photos & Vehicle Clues

- **Multiple branded vehicles** → established fleet, capital intensity, branding investment.
- **New/clean wraps** → recent investment or rebrand (marketing-capital cue).
- **Specialized rigs** (bucket trucks, wreckers, vac trucks, pavers) → high capital intensity → equipment/fleet finance fit.
- **Older/mismatched vehicles** → possible replacement need (equipment-finance angle) — frame as opportunity, not a knock.

---

## 7. Licensing & Registration Clues

- **Registration/incorporation date** → business age. Under ~6-12 months = red-flag penalty per rubric. Multi-year = stability (no penalty; mild positive).
- **Active trade license / bonding** → legitimacy + ability to take bigger/commercial jobs.
- **Contractor license class/scope** → size of jobs they can legally take.
- **DOT/MC number (trucking)** → confirms carrier; authority age = operating history.
- **Lapsed/expired license** → caution flag, "worth verifying."

---

## 8. News & Press

- **Expansion / new location / acquisition** → strong expansion trigger.
- **Large/municipal contract win** → contract + working-capital/project-finance trigger.
- **Awards / "fastest growing"** → growth confirmation.
- **Leadership change** → possible reinvestment phase.
- **Negative press (lawsuits, closures, fraud)** → risk; corroborate before DQ.
- **Only use news that's actually provided** — never invent a headline.

---

## 9. Synthesis: Turning Signals into Output

For each lead, work in this order:
1. **Confirm trade & scope** (website, GBP, license).
2. **Estimate scale** (fleet, team, service area, review volume).
3. **Identify triggers** (jobs, reviews, news, seasonality, region).
4. **Weigh red flags** (age, footprint, distress signals).
5. **Score** per rubric.
6. **Pick product fit** per matrices.
7. **Write the hook** from the single strongest, most concrete signal.

---

## 10. Confidence & Hedge Language

| Evidence Strength | Language to Use |
|---|---|
| Directly stated in enrichment | "confirmed," "they note," state it plainly |
| Strongly implied | "appears," "suggests," "likely" |
| Inferred from trade/region/season | "typically," "this time of year," "most [trade] operators" |
| Thin / single weak signal | "possible," "worth checking" |

**Never** upgrade an inference to a stated fact. If enrichment is empty, fall back to disciplined industry/region/season assumptions and label them — but never pause to ask for more data.

---

## 11. Quick Reference

```
Website → trade, scale, expansion, new lines
Jobs → demand, growth, fleet, expansion (recency = confidence)
Reviews → demand/growth OR distress (patterns, recent, corroborate)
GBP → open/closed, category, volume, activity photos
Fleet pics → capital intensity, equipment/fleet finance fit
License → age, legitimacy, job-size capacity
News → expansion, contracts, risk (only if provided)
RULE: evidence → likely trigger (hedged), never fabricate, never pause
```
