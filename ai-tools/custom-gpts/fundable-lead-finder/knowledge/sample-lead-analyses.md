# Sample Lead Analyses — Optimized Final

**Owner GPT:** Fundable Lead Finder  
**Purpose:** Gold-standard sample outputs for different trades, data-quality levels, score bands, red flags, and batch workflows.

> These are fictional examples. Do not treat any named business as real. Every example follows the required output structure: **1. Research Summary → 2. Trigger Event Analysis → 3. Fundability Score (1-100) → 4. Personalized Outreach Angle.**

## Formatting Rules

- Use the exact four-section structure unless the user requests field-ready CRM output.
- Include a score, score band, and one-sentence justification.
- Use hedge language for inferred data: **likely**, **possible**, **typical**, **worth checking**.
- Do not invent revenue, exact fleet size, ownership, job posts, contracts, or news.
- Keep the outreach angle under 75 words.
- Never pause to ask follow-up questions. Output the best analysis from available data.
- If a lead is hard-disqualified, say so plainly and do not generate outreach.

## Example 1 — Rich Data: Roofing Contractor With Hiring + Expansion

### Input

- Business Name: Apex Roofing Co.
- Industry: Roofing
- Location: Tampa, FL
- Enrichment: 3 open “roofing crew” job posts; website says “now serving Pasco County”; recent reviews mention “booked out 3 weeks.”

### 1. Research Summary

Apex Roofing Co. appears to be a Tampa-based roofing contractor expanding into Pasco County. The crew hiring, service-area expansion, and booked-out reviews point to current demand in a capital-heavy trade where materials, payroll, and insurance-related timing gaps can stack up fast.

### 2. Trigger Event Analysis

- **Hiring spree (confirmed):** Three roofing-crew openings suggest capacity expansion.
- **Service-area expansion (confirmed):** New Pasco County coverage likely requires added crews, marketing, and materials.
- **Storm-season demand (likely):** Tampa roofing demand is often affected by seasonal storms.
- **Cash-flow gap (likely):** Materials and crew payroll often hit before customer or insurance payments clear.

### 3. Fundability Score (1-100)

**Score: 84/100 — Strong Lead.** High-capital roofing operator with confirmed hiring, service-area expansion, booked-out demand, and a natural materials/payroll/receivables bridge angle.

### 4. Personalized Outreach Angle

> Saw you’re adding crews and pushing into Pasco. That kind of growth usually means payroll and materials go out before jobs or insurance checks land. I help roofers compare working-capital and receivables bridge options for that gap. Want me to send a quick example?
